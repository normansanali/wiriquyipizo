Why is The Masters always on Tuesday?

 Posted on July 17, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  523 words  |  Martina Birk

The Masters Tournament, one of the most prestigious events in the world of golf, is renowned for its traditions and unique practices.

Among these traditions is the scheduling of the tournament, which consistently begins on a Thursday, leading many to wonder why the event always starts on a Thursday instead of the more common Friday or Saturday start seen in other golf tournaments.

Origins of The Masters

The Masters Tournament, often referred to simply as The Masters, is one of golf's four major championships and is held annually at the Augusta National Golf Club in Augusta, Georgia.

Founded by golf legend Bobby Jones and investment banker Clifford Roberts, the inaugural tournament took place in 1934. Since then, The Masters has evolved into a celebrated event, attracting top golfers from around the world and captivating audiences with its rich traditions and picturesque setting.

The Tradition of Thursday Start

The decision to start The Masters on a Thursday can be traced back to the tournament's early years. The founders, Jones and Roberts, chose Thursday as the starting day to accommodate the schedules of the participants and patrons.

The tournament's organizers wanted to ensure that players had enough time to familiarize themselves with the course and its nuances before the competition began in earnest.

Course Preparation

Another key factor contributing to the Thursday start is the meticulous preparation of the Augusta National Golf Club course.

The club's staff invests significant time and effort in ensuring that the course is in impeccable condition for the tournament.

Starting the event on a Thursday allows for extensive course maintenance in the days leading up to the competition, ensuring that the playing surface meets the highest standards expected at The Masters.

Practice Rounds and Media Coverage

The days leading up to The Masters are filled with practice rounds, where players fine-tune their strategies and get acclimated to the course.

By commencing the tournament on a Thursday, players have ample time for practice and preparation, enhancing the overall quality of play.

Also, the Thursday start allows for extensive media coverage and buildup, further amplifying the anticipation and excitement surrounding the event.

Tradition and Prestige

The Masters is steeped in tradition and has built a reputation for maintaining its customs and practices.

The Thursday start has become an integral part of the tournament's identity, adding to its allure and mystique.

The adherence to this tradition reflects the reverence for the tournament's history and the desire to uphold the values and principles on which it was founded.

Television Viewership and Attendance

From a broadcasting standpoint, starting The Masters on a Thursday allows for extended television coverage over the weekend, capturing a larger audience and maximizing viewership.

The Thursday start provides an opportunity for patrons and spectators to attend the tournament over the weekend without conflicting with work obligations, potentially boosting attendance and overall fan engagement.

The decision to always start The Masters on a Thursday is rooted in a combination of historical significance, course preparation, tradition, and practical considerations.

This scheduling choice has become an integral part of the tournament's identity and adds to the overall appeal and grandeur of The Masters.
