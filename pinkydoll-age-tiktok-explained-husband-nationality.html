Pinkydoll TikTok Explained

It won’t take long if you watch a live stream video broadcast by PinkyDoll, the content creator on TikTok until you hear her say, “Ice cream so good.” She will keep repeating those words while obnoxiously pretending to lick a cone with her tongue hanging out. She is being paid each time she uses the catchphrase. Now it has become her job.

This month, PinkyDoll rose to fame on social media as a result of her bizarre live streams in which she imitates characters from video games.

During a typical performance, Pinkydoll would squint at the camera and speak a series of scripted lines. Viewers offer her virtual gifts while she streams, such as cartoon roses, dinosaurs, and ice cream cones. For the TikToker, each item represents a cash payment. As the presents appear on the screen, Pinkydoll responds to each one in the same cartoonish way.

As a result of her response to the ice cream cones, many people have started posting pictures of President Biden with his preferred treat and the phrase “Ice cream so good.” Pinkydoll has a sing-song tone to her voice that has been compared to a “sexy baby.” Sometimes she uses a hot-hair flat iron to individually popcorn kernels. The effect, which is tucked away in the uncanny valley, is captivating.

An NPC streamer is what Pinkydoll is referred to as online. NPC, short for “non-player character,” refers to a character in a video game that is preprogrammed and usually unable to be controlled by the user. As a result, an NPC frequently speaks and moves in a predictable and repetitious manner. These fairly mechanical characters are given life by the creator.

One of her notable fans is hip-hop producer Timbaland.

Some people view Pinnkydoll’s actions as having fetish elements. Being able to dictate to her every word and gesture by sending her this or that present may seem sexual to some viewers. Others find her to be utterly intriguing to observe. NPC streaming, according to Carly Kocurek, a professor of game design and experimental media at the Illinois Institute of Technology, should be seen as an extension of cosplay, a pastime in which fans dress as their favorite characters from novels, television shows, and movies.

One of the most well-known NPC streamers in the industry today, Pinkydoll is credited with setting the precedent for the entire thing. She presently has more than 1.8 million views across all of her videos and over 538 thousand followers on TikTok as of this writing.

The 1.5 million-follower TikTok creator Natuecoco is credited with launching the NPC trend. Since PinkyDoll’s recent ascent, several streamers have joined the trend, including Jay Monique and Regena, each of whom has hundreds of thousands of followers. Both people published videos debating PinkyDoll’s popularity but then decided to attempt it on their own accounts, partly as a result of the cash PinkyDoll attracted.

“I was just being cute,” she told the New York Times. “I remember someone saying, ‘Oh my God, you look like an NPC. And then they start sending me, like, crazy money.”

“I was like, ‘I’m going to try to do it like them,’” she said. Still, she added, she is “not really sure” what an NPC is.

It’s fun, she said, coming up with reactions for each gift. “I could sit here all day, but I can’t because I have a son and I got to eat,” she said.
