About Dietrich Mateschitz

Mateschitz was born on May 20, 1944, to a family of Slovene or Croatian ancestry in Sankt Marein im Mürztal, Styria, Nazi Germany, now in Austria.

His father was from Maribor, which is now in Slovenia, and his mother was from Styria. Some sources place his ancestry in the Zadar region, where he allegedly had relatives and the surname Matei was popular. He identified as a cosmopolitan Styrian.

His parents were both teachers, and he spent ten years earning a marketing degree at the Hochschule für Welthandel, which is now known as the Vienna University of Economics and Business. Extreme sports captured his attention and passion at a young age.

In 2017, Mateschitz and his Bundesliga team RB Leipzig came under fire after he advocated for Donald Trump and other populist causes in an interview with Kleine Zeitung. He also suggested that Austria should close its borders to refugees.

He continued by harshly criticizing the governments of Germany and Austria for their actions during the 2015 European refugee crisis.

Mateschitz owned the Fijian island of Laucala in addition to his residence in Fuschl am See, Austria, which he acquired from the Forbes family for £7 million. He purchased and restored homes and castles in the Austrian Alps for millions of dollars.

He battled pancreatic cancer for a long time before passing away on October 22, 2022, at the age of 78.
