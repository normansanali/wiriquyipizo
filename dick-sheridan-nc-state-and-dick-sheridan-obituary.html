Dick Sheridan: The Legacy of a Legendary Coach

Dick Sheridan, a highly esteemed football coach, who transformed the North Carolina State football program and led the Wolfpack to six bowl appearances in seven seasons, passed away on Thursday at the age of 81. His remarkable contributions to the sport will forever be remembered by players, coaches, and fans alike.


1. Introduction: Remembering a Football Legend

Dick Sheridan's legacy in the world of college football is nothing short of legendary. As a coach, he brought about a transformation in the North Carolina State football program, leaving an indelible mark on the Wolfpack's history. This article celebrates his achievements and highlights the impact he had on the sport.

2. Early Life and Career Beginnings

Born with a passion for football, Dick Sheridan embarked on his coaching journey without ever having played the game at the college level. Despite this, he possessed an innate understanding of the sport and a natural ability to lead. Before joining NC State, Sheridan gained valuable experience at Furman University, where he achieved remarkable success, winning eight Southern Conference championships.

3. Taking the Helm at NC State

In 1986, Sheridan assumed the role of head coach at North Carolina State University. The Wolfpack had endured three consecutive 3-8 seasons, and the program was in dire need of a turnaround. Sheridan brought with him a fresh perspective, determination, and a disciplined approach to the game.

4. A Turnaround for the Wolfpack

Under Sheridan's guidance, NC State experienced a dramatic transformation. In his first year as head coach, the Wolfpack finished with an impressive 8-3-1 record, securing a berth in the prestigious Peach Bowl. Although they faced a setback with a 4-7 season in 1987, Sheridan's leadership ensured that the team would never have another losing season during his tenure.

5. Achievements and Accolades

Dick Sheridan's impact on the Wolfpack was profound. During his time as head coach, NC State won the Peach Bowl in 1988 and achieved nine wins in both the 1991 and 1992 seasons. His coaching prowess and dedication were recognized with the Bobby Dodd College Football Coach of the Year award in his inaugural season at NC State. In 2020, Sheridan was deservedly inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame.

6. Retirement and Life After Coaching

In the summer of 1993, Dick Sheridan made the difficult decision to resign from his coaching position at NC State due to health reasons. Despite his retirement from coaching, his impact continued to resonate. Sheridan went on to pursue various ventures, including owning sporting goods stores and working in real estate in the Myrtle Beach area.

7. Conclusion

The passing of Dick Sheridan leaves a void in the world of college football. His unwavering commitment to excellence, his ability to inspire his players, and his undeniable impact on the sport will forever be cherished. As the football community mourns the loss of a true legend, his legacy will live on, inspiring future generations of athletes and coaches.
