Who is Artur Beterbiev next fight?

 Posted on August 16, 2024  |  1 minutes  |  201 words  |  Aldo Pusey

All of the gold is what Artur Beterbiev hopes to hold in light-heavy weight. WBC, IBF, and WBO light-heavyweight champion Anthony Yarde must first defend his titles on January 28 in order for him to advance.

Who is Artur Beterbiev next fight?

In England, Yarde’s backyard, the battle takes place inside Wembley Arena.

Beterbiev vs. Yarde takes place on January 28. The main card starts at 3 p.m. ET | 8 p.m. GMT | 7 a.m. AEDT. Both fighters should make their way to the ring around 5:30 p.m. ET | 10:30 p.m. GMT | 9:30 a.m. AEDT, depending on how long the undercard fights last.

Gold winner at the European Championships twice, Beterbiev (18-0) made his pro debut in 2013. Beterbiev, who fights mostly in Canada, took home the WBA-NABA light-heavyweight championship in 2014. He won the IBF light-heavyweight championship in 2017 after defeating Enrico Kolling.

In 2019, Beterbiev defeated Oleksandr Gvozdyk to win the WBC championship, extending his reign as the best light-heavyweight. Beterbiev won the WBO championship in his final contest against Joe Smith Jr. in June.

Beterbiev has only ever won via knockout. Beterbiev is concentrated on the task at hand despite the possibility of facing WBA champion Dmitry Bivol.
