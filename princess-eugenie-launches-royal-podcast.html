Princess Eugenie's Podcast Is a Royal Family First

 Posted on October 1, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  303 words  |  Aldo Pusey
  • Princess Eugenie of York will launch her own podcast, making her the first member of the British royal family to do so.
  • The podcast is being launched by The Anti-Slavery Collective, a charity that Princess Eugenie cofounded to tackle modern slavery around the world.
  • Eugenie joined Instagram in March 2018 and has regularly posted updates since.

Princess Eugenie of York is one of the few royals to start their own Instagram account, so it's unsurprising that she's also looking for new ways to connect with fans.

Related Story

According to People, Eugenie is about to become the first member of the royal family to launch a podcast, which is more than a little exciting. She will cohost with her close friend Julia de Boinville, who cofounded The Anti-Slavery Collective charity with the princess.

Announcing the news in a video posted to Instagram, Eugenie revealed, "We're developing a podcast with Freedom United, and we will have a speaker series called Tech Tackles Trafficking." She continued, "Now is the time to talk about this, this is on everyone's mind. And if it isn't, we will make it on their mind."

In the Instagram video, which was posted to the official Anti-Slavery Collective account, Eugenie also revealed, "We do this for the person who can't scream and shout like we can â€¦ for the women, girls, men, children all over the world."

By cohosting a podcast, Eugenie hopes the charity's crucial work will reach an even wider audience and, hopefully, make a difference to an issue that still affects millions of people globally.
